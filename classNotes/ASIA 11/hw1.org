Indicate the prominent position of calligraphy in China and East Asia:

Calligraphy as a form of art that is accessible to any literate person cements its place in culture around the world. Among them, areas that have been influenced by ancient Chinese culture are arguably the countries that value calligraphy the most on the societal level.

China, for example, as the civilization formed the writing system most of the Asian writing system branched out from, even include calligraphy into one of the metrics to judge a person. This value passes down dynasty by dynasty, still stuck around after the Liberation. The phrase "字如其人", roughly translate to "one's writing projects one's character", is a prime example of that. In "高考", the most important examination of most of Chinese people's lives, the neatness of the character writing is highly valued and is include into the grading matrix. At the time people still write handwritten applications in order to climb the corporate ladder, calligraphy and overall neatness were large factors to either fail or help you with professional advancements.

Calligraphy has also became one of the major ways for government officials to reinforce foreign relations with each other in East Asian countries. Take the example from the COVID outburst in Wuhan. Several former Japanese Prime Ministers express support and encourage by hand writing calligraphy of encouraging phrase and poetic lines. Same for the medical and everyday supplies sent by the Japanese government, corporations and civilians, hand written words of encouragements are often included.

Above examples represent just a grain of the influence calligraphy has across the Asian countries. Throughout the millennia, calligraphy has deeply soaked into Asian cultures and societies. With the rise of literacy rate in the Asia, what also rose is the art appreciation ability of the general public, specifically calligraphy, as it is simple yet substantive, accessible but elegant.
