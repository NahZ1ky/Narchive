when the boy first drops through the corn, the usual background noises: wind, bugs, distant creatures, drop out. what’s left is the muffled, panicked thrashing as he sinks, and the sound of the corn itself, which is weirdly overwhelming. the silo should be echoing with noise, but instead, everything feels closed off and claustrophobic. when the girl jumps in, the perspective shifts: we get her point of view, and suddenly the soundtrack almost disappears. because the girl is deaf, the film lets us experience the chaos and fear through her silence. it’s not just “quiet”, it’s a complete cut off, like the world outside the corn doesn’t exist anymore.

this silence isn’t peaceful; it’s suffocating. the absence of sound makes every visual detail, the frantic grabbing, the corn swallowing them, feel sharper and more desperate. when the creature shows up, the sound design splits: the little man hears the monster’s terrifying screeches and the metal groaning, but for his sister, it’s still just silence and vibration. the audience knows there’s danger, but she doesn’t, and that disconnect cranks up the tension even more.
