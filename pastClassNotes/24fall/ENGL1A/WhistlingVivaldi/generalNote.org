** overview of the chapter
*** Deficiency as a Partial EXPLANATION:
Initially, social scientists and sportswriters both used deficiency-based EXPLANATIONs
(psychic damage or lack of skill) to account for underperformance, but this doesn’t capture the whole story.
*** Impact of Stigma Pressure:
The research explores the idea that stigma about a group’s intellectual ability
(e.g., women in math, African Americans in academics) can impact performance, creating a psychological burden that goes beyond skill deficiencies.
*** Generalization of Stigma Pressure:
The research initially focused on women in math but expanded to African Americans,
aiming to see if stigma pressure affected intellectual performance across different marginalized groups.
*** Skepticism Regarding Strong Students:
There was skepticism among reviewers about whether strong, motivated black students at prestigious universities could be affected by stigma pressure.
The researchers aimed to test if even high-performing students were vulnerable.
*** Historical Weight and Psychological Burden:
African American students, and others from negatively stereotyped groups, carry the “weight of history” during academic performance.
This psychological burden plays a critical role in their academic outcomes.
